"Calculate pi thanks to random function"

-generation of N points between 0 and 1 randomly (X)
-generation of N points between 0 and 1 randomly (Y)


Let's imagine a circle of diameter 2 and a square with 2 of side, both of them centred at origin

The generated points will be situated in the quarter of circle up-right and the quarter of square up-right.

The area of a circle is pi*r2

The area of a square is (2r)2

so  
pi*r2/(2r)2 = N pts circle/ N pts square

then
pi/4 = N pts cercle/ N pts carré

and we got:

pi = 4*(N pts cercle/ N pts carré)

The more you increase N, the more the pi calculation is accurate. make some tries with N=100, 1000, 10000, 100000...
